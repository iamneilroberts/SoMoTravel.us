Alternative Destinations

Options to Replace Naples in Your Italy Itinerary

Curated Alternatives for Your Journey

Each of these options maintains smooth logistics while offering a unique Italian experience. They all connect seamlessly between Rome and the Amalfi Coast.

1 Option
Extended Stay

Extra Time in Rome + Direct to Amalfi

Skip Naples entirely and savor three full days in the Eternal City instead of two. Dive deeper into Rome's treasures before heading directly to the coast.

Borghese Gallery Appian Way Jewish Ghetto Day Trip to Tivoli

Take the high-speed train from Rome to Salerno, then ferry or private transfer to Positano. Pompeii remains accessible as a day trip from the coast.

Advantages

  • Less packing and unpacking
  • More relaxed pace in Rome
  • Deeper exploration of one city

Considerations

  • Misses distinct destination variety
  • Pompeii becomes day trip only
2 Option
Coastal Charm

Sorrento Base

Replace Naples with two nights in this charming cliffside town perched above the Bay of Naples. Sorrento offers an easier pace, excellent food scene, and the scent of lemon groves around every corner.

Lemon Groves Cliffside Views Walkable Center Easy Pompeii Access

Pompeii is just 20 minutes by Circumvesuviana train. Herculaneum—smaller and less crowded—is also within reach. Ferry connections to Capri and Positano are easy and frequent.

Advantages

  • Relaxed, walkable atmosphere
  • Gorgeous sunset views
  • Great food and limoncello

Considerations

  • More touristy than Naples
  • Less "authentic" grit
3 Option
Unforgettable Detour

Matera — The Ancient Cave City

Swap Naples for one or two nights in this UNESCO World Heritage site. Matera's ancient sassi (cave dwellings) carved into limestone cliffs create one of the most dramatic townscapes in all of Europe.

UNESCO Heritage Cave Hotels Stunning Photography Off the Beaten Path

Route: Rome → Matera (4 hours by car or train+bus) → Amalfi Coast. Stay in a converted cave dwelling for a truly once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Advantages

  • Absolutely unforgettable
  • Dramatic, unique scenery
  • Fewer tourists

Considerations

  • Adds driving time
  • Slightly off the direct route
4 Option
Island Wellness

Ischia Island

Replace Naples with two nights on this volcanic island famous for natural thermal spas and hot springs. More authentic and less crowded than Capri, Ischia offers a true escape.

Thermal Spas La Mortella Gardens Aragonese Castle Beautiful Beaches

Access via ferry from Naples port (you'd pass through briefly) or potentially from Civitavecchia. Enjoy the healing waters, explore the castle, and experience island life before continuing to Positano.

Advantages

  • Relaxing, wellness-focused
  • Authentic local atmosphere
  • Natural thermal experiences

Considerations

  • Requires ferry logistics
  • May overlap with Capri day trip
5 Option
Maximum Relaxation

Extended Amalfi Coast

Skip Naples and head straight to the coast for four nights instead of three. Begin in elegant, mountaintop Ravello before moving to Positano, or simply enjoy more leisure time in one location.

4 Nights on the Coast Ravello's Gardens Unhurried Pace Pompeii Day Trip Option

Day trips to Pompeii remain possible via ferry to Sorrento plus train. This option maximizes your time in one of Italy's most beautiful regions with zero rushing.

Advantages

  • Ultimate coastal relaxation
  • Simpler logistics
  • Flexibility each day

Considerations

  • Misses a distinct destination
  • Less variety overall

Our Recommendation

For most travelers skipping Naples, we suggest Option 2 (Sorrento) or Option 5 (Extended Amalfi Coast) — both keep logistics simple while maintaining easy access to Pompeii if desired.

However, if you're seeking something truly special and unforgettable, Option 3 (Matera) is a showstopper that most tourists never experience. The ancient cave city creates memories that last a lifetime.
