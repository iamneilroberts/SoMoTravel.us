üìã Winter Packing Necessity Checklist

EXTREME COLD LAYERING SYSTEM (0-30¬∞F)

BASE LAYERS (Moisture-Wicking)
  • ‚òê Thermal underwear (long) - merino wool or synthetic (NOT cotton)
  • ‚òê Long-sleeve thermal shirts (2-3 pairs)

OUTER LAYERS (Wind & Weather Protection)
  • ‚òê Waterproof/windproof ski jacket (insulated)
  • ‚òê Waterproof snow pants (insulated)
  • ‚òê Waterproof winter boots (rated for -20¬∞F+)
  • ‚òê Gaiters (prevent snow from entering boots)
HEAD & FACE PROTECTION
  • ‚òê Ski/winter helmet (for skiing - can rent)
  • ‚òê Winter beanie/hat covering ears (wool or thermal)
  • ‚òê Ski goggles (UV protection + visibility in snow)
  • ‚òê Neck gaiter or balaclava (covers face & neck)
  • ‚òê Face mask or scarf (backup for extreme cold)
HANDS & FEET
  • ‚òê Insulated ski gloves or mittens (waterproof, rated for extreme cold)
  • ‚òê Liner gloves (moisture-wicking inner layer)

  • ‚òê Warm pants (jeans with thermal leggings underneath)

  • ‚òê Indoor casual jackets
  • ‚òê Slip-on snow boots for casual outings
  • ‚òê Regular socks (non-thermal, for indoors)
TOILETRIES & PERSONAL
  • ‚òê Lip balm with SPF (prevent chapped lips)

‚ùÑÔ∏è Critical Cold Weather Tips

  • Layer Properly: Three-layer system (base + insulation + protection) is essential. Never wear cotton next to skin.
  • Keep Dry: Wet clothes lose insulation. Change out of wet gear immediately. Bring extra socks.
  • Protect Extremities: Fingers, toes, nose, and ears are most vulnerable. Keep these covered and warm.
  • Stay Hydrated: High altitude + dry winter air = dehydration. Drink water throughout the day.
  • Sunburn Risk: Snow reflects 80% of UV rays. Apply sunscreen daily, even on cloudy days.
  • Frostbite Warning: In -20¬∞F wind chill, exposed skin freezes in 15-30 minutes. Cover all skin.
  • Wind Chill Matters: Temperature + wind speed creates dangerous conditions. Check forecasts daily.
  • Plan Warm Breaks: Schedule indoor time for meals, shopping, and warming up between outdoor activities.

Covered Bridges (Woodstock Area)

Three stunning bridges to visit in and around Woodstock, all within 5-10 minutes of each other. Great for photos and exploring:

Middle Covered Bridge

Built: 1969 | Length: 139 feet

The most photographed bridge in Woodstock, located on Mountain Avenue with views of the village green. Features a pedestrian walkway with western views of a waterfall. Simon Pearce restaurant is nearby for lunch or dinner.

Taftsville Covered Bridge

Built: 1836 | Length: 189 feet

One of the oldest covered bridges in the nation, featuring a modified multiple kingpost truss design. Located on Covered Bridge Road off Route 4, about 5 minutes from Woodstock. Parking available; excellent for sunset photos. Taftsville Country Store nearby for snacks.

Lincoln Covered Bridge

Built: 1877 | Length: 136 feet

National Register of Historic Places with a unique modified Pratt truss design. Located on Route 4 in the Fletcher Hill area, west of Woodstock. The Lincoln Inn & Restaurant is right there for lunch or dinner.

Emily's Bridge

Built: 1844 | Location: Stowe Hollow

Historic covered bridge with local ghost legends, very popular for photos. Closer to your lodge in Stowe if you want a quick bridge visit without traveling to Woodstock.

Suggested Woodstock Route

Start in Woodstock village (Middle Bridge + downtown shopping) ‚Üí Drive 5 min east to Taftsville Bridge + Country Store ‚Üí Drive 10 min west to Lincoln Bridge & Inn. Total driving time between stops: ~20 minutes.
