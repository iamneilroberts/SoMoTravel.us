Enjoy a luxurious 7-night Caribbean cruise aboard Virgin Voyages, visiting 6 beautiful islands with a pre-cruise overnight stay in San Juan, Puerto Rico. This adult-only cruise experience features premium dining venues, innovative entertainment, and immersive shore excursions.

Kim's Hidden Gem

Pack a small "embarkation day bag" with swimwear, sunscreen, and a change of clothes so you can enjoy the ship's amenities while waiting for your luggage to be delivered to your cabin. The Virgin Voyages pools and restaurants open immediately, so you can start your vacation right away!

Other Onboard Dining Venues

In addition to your reserved dining venues, you can also enjoy these restaurants at your leisure (no reservation required):

  • The Galley - Food hall featuring multiple stations with everything from tacos to burgers, pasta, and sushi
  • The Pizza Place - Made-to-order pizzas and flatbreads
  • The Dock House - Mediterranean small plates
  • The Social Club - Comfort food with games
  • The Perch Pool Bar - Casual bites by the pool

Note: While reservations are recommended for dinner at all specialty restaurants, breakfast and lunch are typically open seating at most venues.

Kim's Hidden Gem

Start your day with a quick coffee and pastry at The Grounds Club, Virgin's artisanal coffee shop. Their pour-over coffee is delicious, and the pastries are made fresh daily. Then in the evening, don't miss "The Dock" during sunset hours - it's the perfect spot for a pre-dinner cocktail with amazing ocean views. The Mediterranean-inspired small plates are perfect for sharing!

Essential Items

  • Documents: Passport, cruise documents, flight tickets, travel insurance information
  • Medications: Prescription medications in original containers, basic over-the-counter medicines
  • Clothing: Lightweight casual wear, smart casual attire for dinners, swimwear, cover-ups

  • Reusable Water Bottle: Virgin has filtered water stations throughout the ship
  • Workout Gear: For the ship's extensive fitness offerings
  • Backpack or Tote: For shore excursions and carrying essentials
  • Evening Outfits: Virgin encourages stylish dress but has no formal nights
  • Theme Night Attire: Check the Virgin Voyages app before sailing for any theme night details

For Shore Excursions

  • Beach Bag: For carrying towels and beach essentials
  • Water Shoes: For rocky beaches and water activities
  • Snorkel Gear: If you prefer your own (also available for rent)
  • Underwater Camera: For capturing underwater memories
  • Dry Bag: To protect electronics on water-based excursions
  • Cash: Small denominations for souvenirs and local vendors
Kim's Hidden Gem

Pack a few magnetic hooks for your cabin walls! Virgin's cabins are designed with metal walls, so magnetic hooks can help you organize things like hats, lanyards, or wet swimsuits to dry. Also, bring along a multi-port USB charger to maximize the limited outlets in your cabin - you'll be charging phones, cameras, and other devices throughout your trip.

Caribbean Weather - March

Temperature Range: 75-85°F (24-29°C)

Water Temperature: 78-80°F (25-27°C)

Precipitation: Low chance of rain (March is part of the dry season)

Humidity: Moderate to high

UV Index: Very high - sun protection essential

March offers some of the best weather in the Caribbean with warm temperatures, low rainfall, and comfortable humidity levels. Perfect for beach activities and water sports.

Currency & Spending

Onboard

  • Virgin Voyages is cashless - link a credit card to your account
  • All specialty dining is included in your fare
  • Basic beverages (water, some juices, tea, coffee) included
  • Alcoholic beverages and specialty drinks are additional
  • Gratuities are included in your fare
  • WiFi basic package is included for all sailors

Ports of Call

  • Puerto Rico: US Dollar
  • Tortola (BVI): US Dollar
  • Barbados: Barbadian Dollar (fixed to USD at 2:1 rate); US Dollars widely accepted
  • St. Lucia: Eastern Caribbean Dollar; US Dollars widely accepted
  • Antigua: Eastern Caribbean Dollar; US Dollars widely accepted
  • St. Maarten: US Dollar on Dutch side; Euro on French side; US Dollars accepted everywhere

Tip: Bring small denominations of US dollars for small purchases and tips in ports.

WiFi & Communications

Onboard WiFi

Health & Safety

Medical Facilities

  • Ship has a medical center with doctors and nurses
  • Medical services onboard come with a fee
  • All islands have medical facilities for emergencies

Health Recommendations

  • Bring prescription medications in original containers
  • Pack basic over-the-counter medications
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen and apply frequently
  • Stay hydrated in the Caribbean heat
  • Use insect repellent in ports, especially in evenings

Safety Tips

  • Attend the safety drill at the beginning of the cruise
  • Use ship safe for valuables and travel documents
  • Be aware of your surroundings in ports
  • Use reputable transportation and tour operators

Embarkation & Disembarkation

Check-in Process

  • Complete online check-in via the Virgin Voyages app before sailing
  • Upload a selfie to create your digital "Sailor ID"
  • Select your embarkation time slot during online check-in
  • Arrive at your scheduled embarkation time to avoid crowds

Embarkation Day

  • Drop checked luggage with porters at the terminal
  • Keep essential items in a carry-on bag
  • Have your passport and boarding pass ready
  • Your cabin may not be ready until 3:00 PM
  • Restaurants and pools are open upon boarding

Disembarkation Day

  • Express walk-off option available for those carrying all luggage
  • Luggage tag colors/groups assigned for scheduled departures
  • Vacate cabin by 9:00 AM on final morning
  • Breakfast available in select restaurants
  • Allow at least 2 hours to get from ship to airport

Old San Juan Walking Tour

Recommended walking route (1-2 hours):

  1. Start at Plaza Colón - Central meeting point with the statue of Christopher Columbus
  2. Calle Fortaleza - Walk down this main street with colorful buildings and shops
  3. La Fortaleza/Governor's Mansion - View the beautiful blue exterior of this historic building
  4. Plaza de Armas - Historic main square with a beautiful fountain
  5. Caleta de las Monjas - Picturesque narrow street with great photo opportunities
  6. Paseo de la Princesa - Scenic promenade along the old city walls
  7. Raíces Fountain - Stunning bronze sculpture representing Puerto Rico's heritage
  8. Return to the cruise terminal via Paseo de la Princesa and Calle Marina

Don't Forget for Embarkation

  • Passport
  • Cruise confirmation documents
  • Credit card for onboard expenses
  • Any prescription medications
Kim's Hidden Gem

During your walking tour, stop at Café Manolín (251 Calle San Justo) for a quick and authentic lunch. This local institution serves traditional Puerto Rican favorites like mofongo and tostones in a casual setting. Their sandwiches are perfect for a quick bite before heading to the ship. If you prefer something sweet, Señor Paleta on Calle Tetuan offers gourmet popsicles with tropical fruit flavors—perfect for cooling off during your walk!

Kim's Hidden Gem

If your tour includes free time or ends early, try to visit Smuggler's Cove – a hidden gem beach on the western end of Tortola. This secluded cove has powder-soft sand, excellent snorkeling opportunities right off the beach, and fewer crowds than other popular spots. The Smuggler's Cove Beach Bar serves delicious rum punches and fresh island food with your toes in the sand.
