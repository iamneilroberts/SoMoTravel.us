About St. Maarten

St. Maarten/St. Martin is a unique island divided between Dutch and French territories. The island offers diverse cultural experiences, duty-free shopping, beautiful beaches, and excellent cuisine from both European traditions.

Dining Reservation

Gunbae at 6:15 PM

Korean BBQ restaurant featuring a social, hands-on dining experience with drinking games built into the experience. A festive way to celebrate your last night onboard! Smart casual attire recommended.

St. Maarten Cruise Terminal Recommendations

Historical Sites Within Walking Distance:

  • Fort Amsterdam - Historic Dutch fort with panoramic views (20-25 minute walk)
  • Courthouse - Iconic white and blue building in Philipsburg (10 minute walk)
  • St. Maarten Museum - Small but informative museum about island history (15 minute walk)

Dining Options:

  • Ocean Lounge - Beachfront restaurant with fresh seafood and tropical cocktails
  • Lazy Lizard - Casual beach bar with burgers and local specialties
  • Amsterdam Cheese & Liquor Store - Great for sampling Dutch cheeses and spirits

Shopping Highlights:

  • Front Street - Main shopping area with duty-free jewelry, electronics, and souvenirs
  • Old Street - Charming alley with local crafts and boutiques
  • Guavaberry Emporium - Famous for local liqueur and rum

Kim Recommends: Front Street Bargain Shopping

St. Maarten offers some of the best duty-free deals in the Caribbean. For the best bargains, visit the shops on Front Street after 3:00 PM when many cruise ship passengers have returned to their ships. Merchants are often willing to offer better deals later in the day. The jewelry stores on Front Street are particularly good for deals on watches, diamonds, and tanzanite.
