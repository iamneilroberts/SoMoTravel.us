📍
🚨 CRITICAL: Cable Car vs Water Taxi Guidance
Santorini's Old Port is at the BOTTOM of a 590-foot cliff.
Fira town (where all tours, taxis, and ground transport start) is at the TOP.
You must solve the 'get up the cliff' problem before any tour starts.
THREE OPTIONS: (1) CABLE CAR - €6/person each way, 3-minute ride, BUT queue can be 60-90+ minutes when multiple ships are in port (loads ~36 people every 20 min).
This is the single biggest time-eater on the whole cruise.
(2) DONKEY PATH - 588 zig-zag steps, free if walking.
Shared with donkeys, slippery, smells bad, full sun.
Skip the donkey rides for animal welfare reasons.
(3) WATER TAXI - small boats run €10-20/person from Old Port to Ammoudi Bay (below Oia) - this is the insider alternative.
RETURN TRIP: Cable car queue going DOWN in late afternoon is often WORSE than going up - everyone races back to the ship simultaneously.
If your tour ends in Oia, take a water taxi from Ammoudi Bay directly back to the ship's tender pier to bypass Fira cable car chaos entirely.
When messaging a TBL guide, ask if they can arrange a water taxi return from Ammoudi/Oia.

💡 Milos is best for self-guided exploration
Unlike the busier Greek islands, Milos rewards independent travelers.
Tour options are limited compared to Santorini or Mykonos, but the island is small (~150 km²) and easy to explore by taxi, rental car, or local bus.
Below are the standout free/independent options to fill a 10-hour port day.
📍
Walk Adamas waterfront (right off the tender)
The tender drops you directly into Adamas village.
Walk the harbor promenade - whitewashed houses, tavernas, small shops, fishing boats.
Pop into the Holy Trinity Church (free, 10 min) for traditional Cycladic religious art.
The Mining Museum of Milos is a 5-min walk from the harbor (€4 entry, ~45 min) - small but well-curated, covers the volcanic geology that makes Milos so visually unique.
Easy first hour off the ship while you decide on the day's plan.
📍
Sarakiniko Beach (the 'moonscape')
Famous white volcanic rock formations - looks like a lunar landscape, no other beach in the world looks quite like it.
Short taxi (~€15 each way) or local bus ride from Adamas (~15 min).
FREE, no booking needed.
Bring water shoes if planning to swim - rocks are smooth but sharp at the water's edge.
Best photos in morning light when shadows are long.
Plan ~1.5-2 hours total.
⚠ No shade and no facilities - bring water and sun protection.
📍
Beach-hop the south coast (rental car or taxi day-rate)
Milos has 70+ beaches and many of the best are on the south coast.
If splurging on a taxi day-rate (~€120-180 for a half-day) or quick rental car (~€35-50/day from Adamas agencies), the classic loop is: Sarakiniko → Firopotamos (sheltered cove with photogenic boathouses) → Paliochori (red/orange volcanic cliffs, geothermal sand warm to the touch).
Each stop 30-60 min.
Driving distances are short (15-30 min between beaches).
Combine with lunch at a beach taverna - Sirocco at Paliochori is famous for cooking food in the volcanic sand.
📍
Plaka village (the hilltop capital)
Charming hilltop town with panoramic views over the entire bay.
Where the Venus de Milo statue was discovered in 1820 (the actual statue is in the Louvre, but a replica + small marker on the spot).
Short taxi ride from Adamas (~10 min, €15-20).
Whitewashed Cycladic architecture, narrow car-free lanes, a Venetian castle ruin at the very top with the best sunset view on the island.
Combine with a stop in neighboring Trypiti village for the Catacombs of Milos (early Christian burial site, €4 entry) and the Ancient Theater of Milos (free, panoramic ruins overlooking the sea).
📍
Klima fishing village (Instagram bait)
Tiny seaside village just below Plaka where colorful boathouses ("syrmata") are built right into the cliffs - the ground floor is a boat garage, upper floor is a tiny home.
Each one painted a different bright color.
Walkable down from Plaka (15 min downhill) or taxi from Adamas (~€15).
Photogenic, peaceful, almost no tourists.
30-45 min stop.
⚠ The walk back UP from Klima to Plaka is steep - take a taxi up if hot.
📍
Eat lunch at a Milos taverna (food is the experience here)
Milos cuisine punches above its weight - the local specialty is pitarakia (small cheese pies with mizithra and mint), karpouzopita (watermelon-and-cheese pie, sweet/savory), and pickled caper leaves.
Recommended spots all in or near Adamas/Pollonia: O! Hamos! (touristy but excellent traditional cooking, garden setting), Medusa (seafood by the water in Mandrakia), Astakas (fish taverna in Klima).
Most lunches €20-40 per person.
Reserve ahead in peak season.
📍
Swim at a beach near Adamas (lazy half-day option)
If a full day of touring sounds like too much: walk 15 min from the tender to Lagada Beach or Papikinou Beach - both are organized beaches with sun loungers (€10-15/day), tavernas, and clean swimming.
Not as dramatic as Sarakiniko, but zero stress and you can bail back to the ship anytime.
Perfect after a busy week of cruise port stops.
📍
Ship departs 6:00pm
⚠ Last tender typically 45-60 min before departure.
Plan to be at Adamas port by 5:00pm latest.
The tender pier is right in Adamas village so getting back is easy - just don't be the couple stuck in a taxi from Plaka at 4:45 PM.
