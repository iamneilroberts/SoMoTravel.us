The Experience

A leisurely journey through England's most beautiful canal, combining narrowboat living with e-bike adventures into the Cotswolds

The Boat

4-berth narrowboat with 2 private double cabins and 2 bathrooms. Full galley, central heating, and roof rack for bikes. Departing from Lower Heyford‚Äîtrain station steps from the marina.

The Route

The Oxford Canal, completed in 1790, winds through the landscape rather than cutting through it. South to the dreaming spires of Oxford, north through market towns and Civil War villages.

The Bikes

4 e-bikes for the full 12 days, delivered to the boat. Explore destinations unreachable by canal: Hook Norton Brewery, Blenheim Palace, hidden Cotswolds villages.

The Vibe

Relaxed pace, real ale pubs, farm-to-table dining, quirky village shops, live folk music. No rental cars, no rushing. Authentic England at 4 mph.

Must Do

Hook Norton Brewery

From Banbury moorings
6 miles each way

Famous Victorian tower brewery, still steam-powered. Brewery tours, the Pear Tree pub on-site, and rolling Cotswolds countryside. Book tour in advance.

Must Do

Blenheim Palace & Woodstock

From Oxford moorings
8 miles each way

UNESCO World Heritage Site. Winston Churchill's birthplace. Capability Brown gardens. Woodstock is a charming market town with antiques and pubs.

Must Do

Rousham House & Gardens

From Lower Heyford base
2 miles each way

Beautifully preserved 18th century William Kent gardens with cascades, walled garden, and parterre. Quick morning ride from base.

Deddington

From Lower Heyford
5 miles each way

Larger Cotswolds village with castle ruins, farmers market (4th Saturday), excellent pubs and independent shops.

Burton Dassett Hills

From Fenny Compton
4 miles each way

Panoramic views over Warwickshire, medieval beacon tower, Iron Age hill fort. Best viewpoint of the trip.

Aynho Village

From Aynho Wharf
1 mile each way

Picture-perfect Cotswolds limestone village. Famous for apricot trees growing on cottage walls‚Äîunique microclimate.
