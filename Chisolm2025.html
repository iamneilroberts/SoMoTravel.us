Dublin Self-Guided Walking Tour

▼

Date: Friday, September 19, 2025

Duration: 4-5 hours (with optional extensions)

Distance: Approximately 4 km (2.5 miles)

Difficulty: Easy - mostly flat terrain with occasional steps

Start/End: The Riu Plaza The Gresham Dublin, 23 O'Connell Street Upper

Explore the heart of Dublin on this self-guided walking tour that takes you through the city's historic center. You'll discover iconic landmarks, charming streets, and hidden gems while experiencing the authentic atmosphere of Ireland's capital. This tour is designed at a relaxed pace with plenty of opportunities to stop for coffee, shopping, or to simply soak in the Dublin ambiance.

Tour Highlights

  • Experience the grandeur of Dublin's main thoroughfare, O'Connell Street
  • Cross the iconic Ha'penny Bridge over the River Liffey
  • Explore the cobbled streets of Temple Bar cultural quarter
  • Visit Dublin Castle, the historic heart of the city
  • See the magnificent St. Patrick's Cathedral
  • Enjoy the green oasis of St. Stephen's Green

Recommended Stops

Stop 1: The Riu Plaza The Gresham Dublin

Your tour begins at the historic Gresham Hotel, one of Dublin's most iconic places to stay. Opened in 1817, this landmark hotel on O'Connell Street has hosted countless notable guests throughout its rich history.

Start Time: 9:00 AM

Stop 2: O'Connell Street & The Spire

Walk south down O'Connell Street, Dublin's main thoroughfare. Notice the imposing monument to Daniel O'Connell at the southern end of the street and the striking 120-meter stainless steel spire (officially titled the "Monument of Light") that has become a modern symbol of Dublin.

Duration: 15-20 minutes

Stop 3: General Post Office (GPO)

Visit the iconic General Post Office, one of Ireland's most famous buildings and the focal point of the 1916 Easter Rising. Consider visiting the GPO Witness History exhibition inside to learn more about this pivotal moment in Ireland's path to independence.

Duration: 20-40 minutes

Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM

Admission: GPO Witness History: €14 per adult

Stop 4: Ha'penny Bridge

Continue south to the River Liffey and cross the iconic Ha'penny Bridge (officially the Liffey Bridge). This pedestrian bridge, built in 1816, was the first to cross the Liffey and got its nickname from the halfpenny toll once required to cross it.

Duration: 10-15 minutes

Stop 5: Temple Bar District

Explore the lively Temple Bar district, Dublin's cultural quarter known for its cobbled streets, colorful buildings, and vibrant atmosphere. This is a perfect spot to stop for a mid-morning coffee or snack.

Duration: 30-45 minutes

Kim's Hidden Gem

While in Temple Bar, don't miss The Gutter Bookshop - a charming independent bookstore with a fantastic selection of Irish literature and signed copies from local authors. It's a peaceful retreat from the busier streets and offers a genuine slice of Dublin's literary culture.
