SELF-GUIDED WALK: Arc de Triomphe, Champs-Elysees & Eiffel Tower
TOTAL: Approximately 3.5 to 4 hours of easy, flat walking covering about 3 miles. This walk starts right at your hotel's front door and takes you through the heart of Paris. Wear comfortable shoes. Bring your phone charged with Google Maps downloaded offline. --- STOP 1: ARC DE TRIOMPHE (30-45 minutes) --- Walk out of the hotel and turn LEFT onto Rue La Boetie. Walk one block and turn RIGHT onto Avenue des Champs-Elysees. Walk about 5 minutes up the avenue toward the massive arch you can already see at the top of the hill. You are now at Place Charles de Gaulle, one of the busiest roundabouts in the world with 12 avenues radiating out from the arch. IMPORTANT: Do NOT attempt to cross the road. Look for a brown sign saying 'Arc de Triomphe' pointing to an underground pedestrian tunnel entrance - it is on the right-hand side of the Champs-Elysees as you face the arch. Take the tunnel under the roundabout to reach the monument safely. At the arch: Stand under it and look up at the carved battle scenes. Read the inscriptions. The eternal flame of the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier burns here at all times. If you want to climb to the top for a 360-degree panoramic view of all 12 avenues radiating out from the arch (including a perfect view straight down the Champs-Elysees toward the Louvre), tickets are available on-site or in advance at tickets.monuments-nationaux.fr - approximately 13 EUR per person. The climb is 284 steps with a small elevator partway up. The view is spectacular and worth the 20-25 minutes. --- STOP 2: WALK THE CHAMPS-ELYSEES (30-40 minutes) --- Return through the tunnel and begin walking DOWN the Champs-Elysees away from the arc, heading toward the Louvre in the distance. This is one of the most famous avenues in the world - 1.2 miles long and lined with chestnut trees, luxury boutiques, cafes, and cinemas. As you walk, look across to the center median for photo opportunities with the Arc de Triomphe behind you. The avenue slopes slightly downhill, making for an easy stroll. Pass the Grand Palais on your right (a stunning Beaux-Arts glass and iron exhibition hall built for the 1900 World Fair - worth a glance inside if open). Continue to Place de la Concorde at the bottom - the large square with an ancient Egyptian obelisk in the center, over 3,300 years old, gifted to France in 1833. On a clear day you can see the Eiffel Tower off to your left from here. --- STOP 3: PONT ALEXANDRE III & SEINE RIVERBANK (15-20 minutes) --- From Place de la Concorde, cross to the south side of the avenue and walk along the Seine riverbank heading LEFT (west, toward the Eiffel Tower). After about 5-7 minutes of walking along the river, you will reach Pont Alexandre III - considered the most ornate and beautiful bridge in Paris, decorated with gilded sculptures, cherubs, and ornate lamp posts. Walk across it for a perfect view up and down the Seine. This is one of the best photo spots in the city. --- STOP 4: EIFFEL TOWER & CHAMP DE MARS (45-60 minutes) --- From Pont Alexandre III, continue walking west along the Left Bank (south side of the river) for about 15 minutes. You will see the Eiffel Tower growing larger as you approach - the moment it comes fully into view from the Trocadero gardens is genuinely breathtaking. Cross the river using the Pont d'Iena bridge directly in front of the tower and enter the Champ de Mars park. The Eiffel Tower rises 330 meters and was built in 1889 as the entrance arch for the World Fair - it was originally intended to be temporary. Gustave Eiffel's apartment at the very top is still there. You do not need tickets to stand beneath and around the tower - the base and the park are free. Find a bench in the Champ de Mars park, sit down, and take it all in. This is your first full view of the Eiffel Tower and you have earned it. In May the park is lush and green and filled with picnickers. Note: Do not buy anything from street vendors who approach you in the park. They are persistent - a firm 'Non merci' and walking away is all that is needed. --- RETURN TO HOTEL (20-25 minutes) --- To return to the hotel without retracing your steps, use Uber or Bolt from the Champ de Mars park. Open the app, enter your destination as 'Rochester Champs Elysees, 94 Rue La Boetie, Paris' and a car should arrive within 5-10 minutes. Cost approximately 15-20 EUR. Alternatively, the Metro is nearby: walk to the Bir-Hakeim Metro station (a 5-minute walk from the tower, following the river east). Take Line 6 (Direction: Nation) to Charles de Gaulle - Etoile station (3 stops). Exit and walk 3 minutes back to the hotel.
Evening

You have walked through the heart of Paris on your first afternoon - well done. Head to Le Relais de l'Entrecôte on Rue Marbeuf (a 5-minute walk from the hotel) for a classic first dinner. No reservations taken - arrive before 7 PM to avoid a long wait. The only question they ask is how you want your steak cooked.

Arrive at Gare du Nord - Find Eurostar Departures
STEP-BY-STEP DIRECTIONS INSIDE GARE DU NORD: 1. When you exit the Metro into Gare du Nord, you will enter the main station concourse. It is large and busy - do not panic. Look up and around for signs. 2. Look for signs with a UK FLAG icon. They will say 'Hall 2' or 'Eurostar Departures.' Follow these signs. 3. Take the escalator opposite Platform 17 (or the lifts near Platforms 9-10) UP to the first-floor balcony. The Eurostar check-in area is on this upper level overlooking the main concourse. 4. You will pass through TWO checkpoints before boarding: - First: EES (EU Entry/Exit System) biometric registration - new from late 2025. You may be directed to a terminal to have your photo and fingerprints taken. Follow the instructions and allow extra time for this. - Second: UK Border Force passport control - British officers check your passport and UK ETA here, BEFORE you board the train. Have your passports and UK ETA confirmation ready. - Third: Standard security screening (bags through X-ray, like an airport). 5. After clearing security, you enter the Eurostar departure lounge. There are cafes, a duty-free shop, and seating. Your platform and boarding time will be announced approximately 15-20 minutes before departure. 6. Boarding: Follow signs to your boarding gate (Porte A for cars 11-18, Porte B for cars 1-10). Walk down to the platform via a moving walkway or steps.
