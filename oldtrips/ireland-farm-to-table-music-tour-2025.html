Local Customs

  • Tipping: Not as customary as in the US. 10-15% in restaurants if service not included. Round up taxi fares. No tipping in pubs necessary.
  • Pub Etiquette: Order and pay at the bar (no table service). "Round system" is common - each person buys a round for the group.
  • Greetings: Handshakes are common. "How are you?" or "What's the craic?" (what's happening/what's up) are common greetings.
  • Timing: Irish pace can be more relaxed than American. Service may be slower, particularly in rural areas.

Transportation Guide

  • Driving: Left side of the road. Roundabouts are common and yield to traffic already in the circle.
  • Rural Roads: Often narrow and winding. Allow extra time (30% more than GPS estimates).
  • Fuel: Called "petrol" (gasoline) or "diesel" - be sure to use the correct type.
  • Parking: In cities, use paid car parks when available. "Pay and Display" systems common.
  • Public Transport: Reliable in cities; Dublin has LUAS trams, DART trains, and buses.

Weather Notes

November in Ireland is considered the "low season" with typical temperatures between 40-50°F (4-10°C). Rain is frequent, often as misty drizzle rather than downpours. Daylight hours are limited (sunrise ~7:30am, sunset ~4:45pm), so plan outdoor activities accordingly.

Packing Tips

  • Layered Clothing: Essential for Ireland's changeable weather
  • Waterproof Outer Layer: Rain jacket with hood (umbrellas less practical in wind)
  • Waterproof Footwear: Hiking boots or waterproof walking shoes
  • Quick-dry Materials: For easy washing and drying in accommodations
  • Day Bag: Waterproof backpack for daily essentials
  • Camera: For capturing Ireland's stunning landscapes
  • Hat and Gloves: For cooler days and evenings
  • Reusable Water Bottle: Tap water is safe to drink throughout Ireland
  • Portable Charger: For recharging devices during long days of exploration
