Portugal Packing Guide

What to Wear for Daily Tours & Evening Dinners

March 22–31, 2026

Daytime Tours

Portugal is fairly casual but leans more polished than typical American attire. Here's what works well for sightseeing:

Footwear

  • Comfortable walking shoes are essential—cobblestone streets are everywhere
  • Stylish sneakers or supportive flats work perfectly
  • Avoid flip-flops, which mark you as a tourist

Clothing

  • Well-fitted jeans, chinos, capris, or casual dresses
  • Linen pants are great for warmer days
  • Layers are essential—March temperatures range from 55–65°F
  • Pack a light jacket, cardigan, or versatile scarf
  • Modest tops for church visits—shoulders should be covered

Evening Dinners

Portuguese locals tend to dress up a bit more for dinner, especially in Lisbon and Porto. Smart casual is the standard.

What Works Well

  • A nice blouse or sweater paired with dark jeans or tailored pants
  • A casual dress with a cardigan or blazer
  • Comfortable but stylish low heels, ankle boots, or dressy flats

What to Avoid

  • Athletic wear, shorts, or overly casual clothing at dinner—it stands out

General Tips

  • Stick to neutrals and earthy tones—black, navy, olive, and cream blend in well
  • Portuguese women tend toward classic, understated elegance over flashy
  • A versatile scarf doubles as a style piece and a cover-up for churches
  • Pack pieces that mix and match easily to maximize outfit options
Pro Tip: A lightweight, wrinkle-resistant wardrobe will serve you well. Choose fabrics that travel easily and can be dressed up or down!
