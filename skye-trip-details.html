Day 1: Journey to Skye & Southern Exploration

Friday, April 25, 2025

Morning

09:00: Check out of 20a, By The Ness apartment in Inverness

09:00-10:30: Drive from Inverness to Eilean Donan Castle (70 miles, approx. 1 hr 30 min)

10:30-12:00: Visit Eilean Donan Castle (accessible for Mary)

  • Historic castle on a small tidal island
  • Featured in numerous films including "Highlander"
  • Picturesque setting with mountain backdrop
  • Well-maintained paths suitable for Mary

Lunch (12:00-13:00)

Eilean Donan Castle Café or Clachan Bar in Dornie

  • Castle café offers soups, sandwiches, and Scottish specialties
  • Clachan Bar serves traditional pub fare with local ingredients

Afternoon

13:00-13:30: Drive from Eilean Donan to Kyleakin (9 miles, approx. 20 min)

13:30-14:00: Stop at Kyleakin to see Castle Moyl ruins

  • Brief viewpoint stop (10-15 min)
  • Historic ruins of 14th-15th century castle
  • Good views across to the mainland
  • Accessible viewing from harbor area

14:00-14:15: Drive Kyleakin to Broadford (7 miles, approx. 15 min)

14:15-15:15: Stop in Broadford

  • Stretch legs in town
  • Coffee at Café Sia (well-regarded local café)
  • Brief exploration of the village

15:15-15:45: Drive Broadford to Aurora B&B (12 miles, approx. 25 min)

15:45-17:00: Check in to Aurora B&B and rest

Evening

17:00-17:30: Drive to Carbost (5 miles, approx. 10-15 min)

18:00-19:30: Dinner at Caora Dhubh Coffee Company or Taigh Ailean (Talisker Inn)

  • Caora Dhubh: Casual dining with local ingredients in converted farm building
  • Taigh Ailean: Traditional pub food with local seafood, popular with locals

19:30-20:15: Optional evening drive to Talisker Bay viewpoint for sunset (4 miles from Carbost)

  • Short walk from car park, but Mary can enjoy sunset colors from car if preferred
  • Beautiful black sand beach with sea stacks
  • Often less crowded than other Skye attractions

20:15-20:30: Return to Aurora B&B (9 miles, approx. 20 min)

Day 2: Northern Skye Exploration

Saturday, April 26, 2025

Morning

09:00: Depart Aurora B&B

09:00-09:45: Drive to Dunvegan area (18 miles, approx. 35-40 min)

09:45-10:30: Breakfast at The Pier Café in Dunvegan or Café Lephin in Glendale

  • The Pier Café: Casual café with breakfast options and waterfront views
  • Café Lephin: Small café with breakfast, coffee and baked goods

10:30-12:30: Visit Dunvegan Castle & Gardens

  • Oldest continuously inhabited castle in Scotland (800+ years)
  • Home to the famous Fairy Flag
  • Beautiful walled garden and woodland walks
  • Well-maintained paths suitable for Mary
  • Allow 2 hours for both castle and gardens

Lunch (12:30-13:30)

Dunvegan Castle café or Loch Bay Restaurant (15 min drive to Stein)

  • Castle café: Casual dining with soups, sandwiches and Scottish fare
  • Loch Bay Restaurant: Seafood specialist in the village of Stein

Afternoon

13:30-14:15: Drive from Dunvegan to Uig (15 miles, approx. 30 min)

14:15-14:35: Brief stop at Skye Brewing Company in Uig

  • Sample local craft beers (Sky Blue, Sky Red, Sky Gold, Sky Black)
  • Recently added gin production
  • Small tasting room with views of Uig Bay

14:35-15:15: Drive from Uig to Kilmuir via Staffin (18 miles, approx. 35 min)

15:15-16:00: Visit Skye Museum of Island Life

  • Collection of thatched cottages showing island life up to the 1950s
  • Relatively accessible paths between buildings
  • Flora MacDonald's grave monument nearby
  • Highland cattle may be visible (owner can call them over)
  • Café with excellent coffee and homemade cakes

16:00-16:15: Drive to Staffin Dinosaur Museum (3 miles, approx. 10 min)

16:15-17:00: Visit Staffin Dinosaur Museum

  • £4 per adult admission
  • Accessible facility with impressive fossil collection
  • Includes world's smallest dinosaur footprint (1.7cm)
  • Run by Dugald Ross who found many of the exhibits

17:00-17:30: Visit Staffin Beach (dinosaur footprints)

  • Visible at low tide (check tide times in advance)
  • Neil and Kim can walk down to see prints while Mary rests in car
  • 170 million-year-old fossilized prints
  • Alternative viewing spot may be available from parking area

17:30-17:40: Drive to Kilt Rock viewpoint (2 miles, approx. 5-10 min)

17:40-18:00: Kilt Rock & Mealt Falls viewpoint

  • Easily accessible from car park
  • 90m/300ft cliff face of basalt columns resembling a kilt
  • Waterfall plunges from cliff to sea
  • Great photo opportunity

Evening

18:00-19:00: Drive from Kilt Rock back toward accommodation area (35 miles, approx. 1 hour)

19:00-20:30: Dinner at The Three Chimneys in Colbost or Edinbane Lodge

  • The Three Chimneys: High-end dining with local ingredients (reservation essential)
  • Edinbane Lodge: Fine Scottish cuisine in historic building (reservation required)

20:30-21:00: Return to Aurora B&B (distance varies: 12 miles/25 min from Edinbane or 7 miles/15 min from Colbost)

Day 3: Return to Inverness via Portree

Sunday, April 27, 2025

Morning

09:00: Check out of Aurora B&B

09:00-09:45: Drive to Broadford (12 miles, approx. 25 min)

09:45-10:30: Breakfast at Cafesia in Broadford

  • Café with good breakfast options and coffee

10:30-11:00: Drive from Broadford to Portree (25 miles, approx. 35 min)

11:00-12:00: Visit Portree harbor

  • Colorful buildings and scenic harbor
  • Accessible short walk along waterfront
  • Beautiful views of surrounding hills
  • Portree is the largest town on Skye
  • Relax at harbor-side café while taking in the views

Lunch (12:00-13:15)

Café Arriba or Scorrybreac Restaurant in Portree

  • Café Arriba: Casual dining with soups, sandwiches and local fare
  • Scorrybreac: Higher-end restaurant with excellent seafood (may need reservation)

Afternoon

13:15-13:45: Drive from Portree to Old Man of Storr car park (7 miles, approx. 15 min)

13:45-14:45: Stop at Old Man of Storr car park

  • Mary can enjoy the view from car
  • Neil and Kim could take a 30-45 minute quick hike if desired
  • 160-foot/50m rock pinnacle visible from parking area
  • One of Skye's most iconic landscapes

14:45-15:15: Drive to Sligachan (10 miles, approx. 20 min)

15:15-15:45: Stop at Sligachan

  • Beautiful mountain views accessible from car park
  • Historic bridge and hotel
  • Great photo opportunity of the Cuillin mountains
  • River Sligachan with legend of eternal beauty

15:45-16:15: Drive back toward Struan/Ebost area (15 miles, approx. 25 min)

16:15-17:00: Optional: Visit Dunbeg Broch near your accommodation

  • 10-minute uphill walk for Neil and Kim while Mary rests in car
  • Iron Age fort with spectacular views over Loch Bracadale
  • About 3,000 years old
  • One of the best preserved brochs on Skye

17:00-19:30: Journey back to Inverness (115 miles, approx. 2.5 hours)

  • Drive via Broadford, Kyle of Lochalsh, and Loch Ness
  • Possible brief stops at viewpoints along the way as time permits
  • Check into City Center VRBO apartment in Inverness

Evening

20:00: Dinner in Inverness

Driving Distance Summary

  • Day 1: Inverness to Aurora B&B via Eilean Donan Castle: ~100 miles (3 hours total driving)
  • Day 2: Skye northern loop: ~110 miles (3.5 hours total driving)
  • Day 3: Aurora B&B to Inverness via Portree: ~175 miles (4.5 hours total driving)

Additional Information

For these popular restaurants, advance reservations are strongly recommended:

  • The Three Chimneys (Colbost)
  • Edinbane Lodge
  • Scorrybreac Restaurant (Portree)
  • Loch Bay Restaurant (Stein)
Weather Contingency Plans

If weather is poor, consider these indoor alternatives:

  • Extended visit to Dunvegan Castle interior
  • Talisker Distillery tour (if available)
  • Additional time at Staffin Dinosaur Museum
  • More time in cafés and restaurants
  • Aros Centre in Portree (if open)
Local Specialties to Try
  • Skye Brewery beers (Sky Blue, Sky Red, Sky Gold, Sky Black)
  • Talisker whisky
  • Fresh seafood (especially at Loch Bay Restaurant)
  • Local lamb dishes
  • Cullen skink (traditional Scottish soup)
  • Haggis (for the adventurous)
  • Cranachan (traditional Scottish dessert)
