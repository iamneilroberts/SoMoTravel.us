McLeod Heritage Journey: Highlands, Skye & Scenic Rail

7 Days • Inverness Home Base • No Driving Required

This adventure blends comfort and heritage: you’ll be based in Inverness, with small‑group day tours and train journeys unlocking Skye, Loch Ness, and the Highlands. It feels like a luxury holiday, but without large tour buses or complicated logistics. Clan connections are woven in, with visits to Dunvegan Castle and Culloden Battlefield.

Total Package

$4,530 for 2 Travelers (about $2,265 per person)

What’s Included

  • Flights Gulfport → Inverness (with connections)
  • 5 nights at Kingsmills Hotel (Deluxe room, breakfast included)
  • Skye 2‑day / 1‑night small‑group tour (Portree overnight)
  • Loch Ness full‑day small‑group tour (with cruise & Urquhart Castle)
  • Train Day: choose from gentle heritage steam to adventurous scenic lines

Day‑by‑Day Itinerary

Day 1: Depart Gulfport around noon (overnight flight)

Day 2: Arrive Inverness ~9am, settle in. Explore Inverness Museum, Cathedral, Ness Islands. Optional: Culloden Battlefield Visitor Centre.

Day 3: Skye Tour Day 1 — travel via Eilean Donan Castle, arrive Portree. Overnight in Portree.

Day 4: Skye Tour Day 2 — explore Fairy Pools, Quiraing, Dunvegan Castle. Return to Inverness in the evening.

Day 5: Loch Ness full‑day tour — cruise Loch Ness, visit Urquhart Castle, enjoy Highland villages.

Day 6: Train Day — choose your adventure:
• Gentle: Strathspey Steam Railway with Boat of Garten stop (easy walking, afternoon tea)
• Moderate: Highland Main Line to Pitlochry (Victorian town, distillery tasting, riverside walk)
• Adventurous: Kyle Line to Kyle of Lochalsh (2.5 hrs each way, mountains and lochs, Skye views)
• Epic: Far North Line to Wick/Thurso (4 hrs each way, remote and wild northern Highlands)

Day 7: Fly home from Inverness

Highlights & Heritage Connections

  • Dunvegan Castle — ancestral seat of Clan MacLeod and home of the Fairy Flag.
  • Culloden Battlefield — walk the ground where Highland history changed forever.
  • Loch Ness — scenic cruise and visit to the ruins of Urquhart Castle.
  • Skye’s Landscapes — Fairy Pools, Quiraing, and colorful Portree harbor.
  • Rail Adventures — from nostalgic steam to epic wilderness lines, all simple to board in Inverness.

Why It’s Easier Than You Think

No driving is required — every excursion is guided or rail-based. You’ll only pack overnight once (Portree on Skye). Hotels are comfortable, tours are limited to small groups, and rail journeys are straightforward from Inverness Station. For those who haven’t been overseas before, this itinerary is designed to be approachable, relaxing, and memorable.

Pricing reflects flights + hotel package and listed tours only. Meals, admissions, and personal expenses are not included. Itinerary may adjust with weather and seasonal opening times (e.g., Dunvegan Castle is open spring–autumn).
