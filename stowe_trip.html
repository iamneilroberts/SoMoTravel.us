Covered Bridges (Woodstock Area)

Three stunning bridges to visit in and around Woodstock, all within 5-10 minutes of each other. Great for photos and exploring:

Middle Covered Bridge

Built: 1969 | Length: 139 feet

The most photographed bridge in Woodstock, located on Mountain Avenue with views of the village green. Features a pedestrian walkway with western views of a waterfall. Simon Pearce restaurant is nearby for lunch or dinner.

Taftsville Covered Bridge

Built: 1836 | Length: 189 feet

One of the oldest covered bridges in the nation, featuring a modified multiple kingpost truss design. Located on Covered Bridge Road off Route 4, about 5 minutes from Woodstock. Parking available; excellent for sunset photos. Taftsville Country Store nearby for snacks.

Lincoln Covered Bridge

Built: 1877 | Length: 136 feet

National Register of Historic Places with a unique modified Pratt truss design. Located on Route 4 in the Fletcher Hill area, west of Woodstock. The Lincoln Inn & Restaurant is right there for lunch or dinner.

Emily's Bridge

Built: 1844 | Location: Stowe Hollow

Historic covered bridge with local ghost legends, very popular for photos. Closer to your lodge in Stowe if you want a quick bridge visit without traveling to Woodstock.
