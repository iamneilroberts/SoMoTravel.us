Recommended: Edinburgh Castle: Guided Walking Tour with Entry Ticket

Skip the lines and explore Scotland's most famous fortress - More Info

Perched on Castle Rock, Edinburgh Castle has dominated the skyline for over 900 years. Your expert guide brings history to life:

  • The Crown Jewels - Scotland's glittering crown, scepter, and sword of state
  • The Stone of Destiny - Ancient coronation stone of Scottish kings
  • St. Margaret's Chapel - Edinburgh's oldest building dating from 1130
  • The Great Hall - Magnificent banquet hall with hammerbeam roof
  • Mons Meg - Massive 15th-century siege cannon
  • One O'Clock Gun - Still fired daily (except Sundays)

Guided tour lasts 1 hour, then explore at leisure. Panoramic views stretch from Arthur's Seat to the Forth Bridge. Audio guides in 12 languages. Most areas accessible.

Thursday, Apr 16 Day 11 - Limerick Local Treasures

Morning: King John's Castle (5-minute walk from hotel)

Recommended: St. Mary's Cathedral
Founded in 1168, this ancient cathedral holds fascinating secrets. Find the Leper's Squint where medieval lepers watched Mass. See priceless manuscripts and a 400-year-old chained Bible. The 15th-century misericords feature 23 black oak seats with mythical creatures and Ireland's only medieval mermaid carving.

Lunch: House Limerick - www.houselimerick.ie

Afternoon: Hunt Museum & River Shannon boardwalk
The Hunt Museum houses Ireland's finest private collection including Picasso, Renoir, and Jack B. Yeats, plus ancient artifacts and the coin said to be one of Judas's thirty pieces of silver. The level River Shannon boardwalk offers gentle walking with benches and swan watching.

Evening: Farewell dinner at hotel's River Restaurant
