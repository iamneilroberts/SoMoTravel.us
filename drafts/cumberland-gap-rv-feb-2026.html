Museum of Appalachia
Smithsonian Affiliate living history museum on 65 acres. Features 35 historic log cabins, barns, farm animals, churches, schools, and gardens. Over 250,000 artifacts including folk art, musical instruments, baskets, quilts, and Native American artifacts. Plan 3-4 hours to explore thoroughly. The Mark Twain family cabin is a highlight.
More Info →
13:00
Lunch at Museum Restaurant
On-site restaurant specializing in Southern Appalachian country cooking. Daily lunch specials 11am-2pm featuring casseroles, oven fried chicken, fried apples, mashed potatoes, and homemade desserts. Every meal comes with cornbread!
14:30
Norris Dam State Park
First dam built by TVA (1936). Visit the Lenoir Museum Cultural Complex with Appalachian artifacts and pre-dam history. See the 18th Century Rice Grist Mill and Crosby Threshing Barn. Drive across the dam for views.
