Local Customs & Etiquette

Greeting Etiquette

In France, it's customary to greet shopkeepers with 'Bonjour' when entering and 'Au revoir' when leaving. In Italy, 'Buongiorno' (good morning/day) or 'Buonasera' (good evening) works similarly.

‚úì Do: Say 'Bonjour/Buongiorno' when entering shops, restaurants, or elevators

‚úó Avoid: Walking into a shop and immediately asking for something without greeting first

Dining Pace

Europeans dine leisurely. In both Paris and Rome, a meal is an experience‚Äîexpect 1.5-2 hours minimum. Waiters won't rush you or bring the check until you ask.

‚úì Do: Signal for the check when ready by saying 'L'addition' (France) or 'Il conto' (Italy)

‚úó Avoid: Expecting the check to arrive automatically‚Äîit's considered rude to rush diners

Church Dress Code

Both countries require covered shoulders and knees in churches. St. Peter's and the Vatican are strict; Notre-Dame area churches are more relaxed but still respectful.
