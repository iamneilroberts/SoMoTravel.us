Local Customs & Etiquette

Haggling & Tipping

Bargaining is expected at market stalls and with taxi drivers. Start at 50% of the asking price. Tipping 10-15% is appreciated at restaurants.

‚úì Do: Negotiate politely at craft markets‚Äîit's part of the culture

‚úó Avoid: Paying the first price quoted at non-fixed-price shops

British Manners

Grand Cayman is a British territory. 'Please' and 'thank you' go a long way. Driving is on the left side. Tipping 15-18% is standard at restaurants.

‚úì Do: Queue patiently‚Äîcutting in line is considered very rude

‚úó Avoid: Being overly casual with service staff; politeness is valued

Safety & Interaction

Jamaicans are friendly and welcoming. Stick to organized excursions or the port area. Aggressive vendors may approach‚Äîa firm 'No, thank you' works.

‚úì Do: Book ship excursions for Jamaica‚Äîsafer and more convenient

‚úó Avoid: Venturing far from port independently; stick to organized tours

Ship Etiquette

Common cruise courtesies: don't save lounge chairs with towels for hours, tip your cabin steward and dining staff at cruise end, and be on time for all-aboard!

‚úì Do: Pre-pay gratuities or tip $12-15 per person per day at cruise end

‚úó Avoid: Being late to the ship‚Äîthey WILL leave without you!
