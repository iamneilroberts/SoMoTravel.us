Day-by-Day Itinerary

1

Drive from South Mississippi to first overnight location. Depending on route, either Norris/Clinton TN area (if taking I-65 to I-40) or Cherokee NC area (if taking I-59 to I-75 to US-74).
Travel day
Leisurely drive with stops as needed. Arrive in time for dinner at a local restaurant.
2

Norris, Tennessee
Full day at the crown jewel of Appalachian living history museums.
Museum of Appalachia
65-acre Smithsonian Affiliate with 35+ authentic log cabins, 250,000+ artifacts, working farm with animals. Wander at your own pace through the village, display barn, and Hall of Fame.
More Info ‚Üí
Norris Dam State Park
Optional easy drive to see the historic TVA dam and visitor center if time permits
3

Scenic drive day from East Tennessee to Cherokee, NC. Multiple route options depending on energy and weather.
Scenic drive to Cherokee
Options: (A) US-441 through Gatlinburg and over Newfound Gap - dramatic mountain scenery, (B) Foothills Parkway to Townsend then US-321/19 - less traffic, pretty streams, or (C) Blue Ridge Parkway sections if weather is clear
Mountain Farm Museum
Collection of historic log buildings - farmhouse, barn, apple house, springhouse. Free admission. Hard-packed gravel paths, wheelchair accessible with assistance.
More Info ‚Üí
4

Cherokee, NC
Morning at the Cherokee living history village, afternoon for rambling.
Oconaluftee Indian Village
Replica 18th-century Cherokee village. Guided tours with live craft demonstrations by Cherokee artisans - beadwork, basket weaving, woodcarving, pottery, dugout canoe making, blowgun demonstrations. Shaded paths, 2-3 hours.
More Info ‚Üí
Qualla Arts and Crafts Mutual
Oldest Native American cooperative in the country. Browse authentic Cherokee crafts - excellent baskets, woodcarvings, pottery.
Drive along Oconaluftee River
Scenic rambling along the river through the Qualla Boundary
5

Bryson City, NC
Train ride day! The Tuckasegee River or Nantahala Gorge excursion.
Great Smoky Mountains Railroad
Scenic train excursion from historic Bryson City depot. Choose Tuckasegee River Excursion (4 hours, 32 miles) or Nantahala Gorge Excursion (4.5 hours, 44 miles with layover at gorge). First class dining available.
More Info ‚Üí
Smoky Mountain Trains Museum
Included with train ticket. One of largest private Lionel train collections. Good for before or after the train ride.
Downtown Bryson City
Browse the quaint downtown shops and galleries
6

Townsend, TN
Morning drive to Townsend, afternoon at Cades Cove.
Scenic drive to Townsend
Drive US-441 through Great Smoky Mountains NP via Newfound Gap (if not done on Day 3), or take the quieter route via US-19 to US-129. About 1.5-2 hours.
Cades Cove Loop
11-mile one-way loop through historic valley with preserved 19th-century homesteads, churches, grist mill. Stop at pull-offs to view cabins and wildlife (deer, turkeys, often bears). Allow 2-4 hours depending on stops.
More Info ‚Üí
7

Townsend area
Relaxed day for scenic drives, easy walks, and unhurried exploration.
Little River Road scenic drive
Beautiful drive along Little River from Townsend into the park. Numerous pull-offs for viewing the mountain stream. Can drive to Elkmont or Sugarlands.
Return to Cades Cove
If you want more time at Cable Mill or other sites, or to see morning wildlife
Tuckaleechee Caverns
If the group enjoys caverns - impressive formations, but requires walking/stairs
Great Smoky Mountains Heritage Center
Local history museum with outdoor structures, if looking for more heritage content
More Info ‚Üí
8

Drive home to South Mississippi. Can take scenic route via Chattanooga or direct route via I-40/I-65.
Travel home
Leisurely drive with stops as needed. Approximately 6-7 hours to South Mississippi depending on specific location.
