Day-by-Day Itinerary

1

Clinton, Tennessee
Leisurely drive from South Mississippi to the Norris/Clinton area. Approximately 400-450 miles, 6.5-7.5 hours driving time (plan 8-9 hours with breaks).
Depart South Mississippi
Route: I-59 N to Birmingham ‚Üí I-65 N to Nashville ‚Üí I-40 E to Knoxville ‚Üí TN-61 N to Norris/Clinton. Fill up before leaving and keep snacks/water in the car.
Break Stop: Birmingham area
About 2-2.5 hours from home. I-65 Exit 260-262 has Cracker Barrel. For a special stop, detour 20 minutes to Irondale Cafe ‚Äî the real 'Whistle Stop Cafe' from the book/movie.
Break Stop: Franklin/Cool Springs, TN
About 4-4.5 hours from home. I-65 Exit 68-69. Puckett's Grocery has great Southern food. Historic downtown Franklin is charming for a leg stretch.
Break Stop: Cookeville, TN
About 1.5 hours past Nashville. I-40 Exit 286-290. Good lunch spot ‚Äî Cream City Ice Cream is a local favorite.
Arrive Clinton & Dinner
Check into Hampton Inn Clinton. For dinner, try Harrison's Grill & Bar in Clinton (good food, outdoor seating, locals love it) or drive 20 minutes to Calhoun's on the Lake in Oak Ridge for waterfront BBQ and ribs.
2

Norris, Tennessee
Full day at the crown jewel of Appalachian living history museums. Just 10 minutes from your hotel.
Museum of Appalachia
65-acre Smithsonian Affiliate with 35+ authentic log cabins, 250,000+ artifacts, working farm with heritage breed animals. Wander at your own pace through the village, display barn, and Hall of Fame. Don't miss the folk art collection and musical instruments display.
More Info ‚Üí
Lunch at Museum Restaurant
Eat at the on-site Museum of Appalachia Restaurant ‚Äî Southern Appalachian cooking with casseroles, hearty entrees, and homemade desserts. Often dulcimer music playing. The natural choice for lunch.
Norris Dam State Park
Optional easy drive to see the historic TVA dam (first major TVA project, 1936) and visitor center. Pretty views, easy walking.
Dinner options
The Vols Diner in tiny Norris is charming for classic American comfort food. Or return to Harrison's Grill & Bar in Clinton if you enjoyed it last night.
3

Cherokee / Bryson City, NC
Scenic drive day from East Tennessee to the North Carolina mountains. About 100-130 miles, 2.5-3.5 hours depending on route and stops.
Route Option A: Newfound Gap (Recommended for first-timers)
I-40/75 S through Knoxville ‚Üí US-441 S through Gatlinburg ‚Üí Newfound Gap Road through Great Smoky Mountains NP ‚Üí Cherokee. About 3 hours. Spectacular mountain scenery but curvy. Stop at Sugarlands Visitor Center for restrooms and info.
Route Option B: Quieter Townsend Route
US-321 S to Maryville ‚Üí US-321 E to Townsend ‚Üí Little River Road through GSMNP ‚Üí US-441 to Cherokee. About 3-3.5 hours. Less traffic than Gatlinburg, pretty drive along Little River.
Break Stop: Maryville, TN
About 45 minutes from Clinton. Good spot for coffee or restroom ‚Äî Starbucks and local cafes available.
Mountain Farm Museum (Free)
At Oconaluftee Visitor Center near Cherokee. Collection of historic log buildings ‚Äî farmhouse, barn, apple house, springhouse. Hard-packed gravel paths. Quick 30-45 minute stop.
More Info ‚Üí
Check in & Dinner in Bryson City
Check into Stonebrook Lodge (downtown, walking distance to train depot). For dinner, try Jimmy Mac's on Main Street ‚Äî casual and inviting with soups, steaks, and seafood. Or splurge at The Bistro at The Everett Hotel for farm-to-table Southern cuisine (reservations recommended).
4

Cherokee, NC
Morning at the Cherokee living history village, afternoon for rambling. Cherokee is just 10 miles from Bryson City (15-20 minute drive).
Oconaluftee Indian Village
Replica 18th-century Cherokee village with guided tours and live craft demonstrations by Cherokee artisans ‚Äî beadwork, basket weaving, woodcarving, pottery, dugout canoe making, blowgun demonstrations. Shaded paths make it pleasant even in warm weather.
More Info ‚Üí
Lunch: Paul's Family Restaurant
Cherokee local favorite serving buffalo burgers and Indian Tacos on fry bread. Casual, affordable, open 7 days. A taste of Cherokee culture.
Qualla Arts and Crafts Mutual
Oldest Native American cooperative in the country (est. 1946). Browse authentic Cherokee crafts ‚Äî excellent baskets, woodcarvings, pottery. Worth a stop for unique souvenirs.
Drive along Oconaluftee River
Scenic rambling along the river through the Qualla Boundary. Pretty pull-offs and easy photo stops.
Dinner back in Bryson City
River's End Restaurant at Nantahala Outdoor Center (between Cherokee and Bryson City) has a beautiful riverbank setting and locally sourced menu. Or return to a Bryson City favorite.
5

Bryson City, NC
Train ride day! The depot is walking distance from Stonebrook Lodge.
Great Smoky Mountains Railroad
Scenic train excursion from historic Bryson City depot. Choose Tuckasegee River Excursion (4 hours, 32 miles) or Nantahala Gorge Excursion (4.5 hours, 44 miles with layover at gorge ‚Äî crosses Fontana Lake on historic trestle bridge). First class includes meal and beverage service.
More Info ‚Üí
Smoky Mountain Trains Museum
Included with train ticket. One of the largest private Lionel train collections. Good for before or after the train ride ‚Äî right at the depot.
Downtown Bryson City stroll
Browse the quaint downtown shops and galleries. The town is very walkable from the lodge and depot.
Lunch
If riding first class, lunch is served on the train. Otherwise, grab lunch in Bryson City before or after ‚Äî Mountain Java has great wraps and coffee.
Farewell dinner in Bryson City
Last night in this area! The Bistro at Everett Hotel is the top choice for a memorable farm-to-table meal (reservations recommended). Jimmy Mac's is reliable for a casual option.
6

Townsend, TN
Morning drive to Townsend (60-75 miles, 1.5-2.5 hours), afternoon exploring Cades Cove.
Route to Townsend (Recommended: Western Route)
US-19 W to US-74 W to US-129 N ‚Üí TN-28 to US-321 to Townsend. About 2 hours. Avoids Gatlinburg/Pigeon Forge tourist traffic. Pretty route through Nantahala area. NOTE: Do NOT take the 'Tail of the Dragon' section of US-129 (318 curves in 11 miles) ‚Äî take the safer TN-28 route instead.
Check in at Highland Manor Inn
Family-owned since 1982. Peaceful hilltop setting, exceptionally clean. Request ground floor room when booking. Free continental breakfast with homemade muffins. Two miles from park entrance.
Cades Cove Loop
11-mile one-way loop through historic valley with preserved 19th-century homesteads, churches, and grist mill. Stop at pull-offs to view cabins and wildlife (deer, turkeys, often bears). Cable Mill area has working grist mill and multiple historic buildings.
More Info ‚Üí
Dinner in Townsend
Dancing Bear Appalachian Bistro is THE farm-to-table destination ‚Äî Chef Jeff Carter (formerly of Blackberry Farm) uses produce grown on-site. Sweet tea brined pork chop is the signature dish. Reservations required. For casual, try Riverstone Family Restaurant (comfort food, good biscuits).
7

Townsend area
Relaxed day for scenic drives, easy walks, and unhurried exploration. No set schedule ‚Äî go where the day takes you.
Little River Road scenic drive
Beautiful drive along Little River from Townsend into the park. Numerous pull-offs for viewing the mountain stream. Can drive to Elkmont historic district or continue to Sugarlands Visitor Center.
Return to Cades Cove (morning)
If you want more time at Cable Mill, to see morning wildlife, or explore buildings you missed yesterday. Early morning (before 10 AM) has less traffic and more active wildlife.
Great Smoky Mountains Heritage Center
Local history museum in Townsend with outdoor historic structures. Good option if looking for more heritage content without a long drive.
More Info ‚Üí
Tuckaleechee Caverns
Impressive cave formations if the group enjoys caverns. Note: requires walking and some stairs, so assess mobility comfort first.
Lunch options
Little River Pub & Deli has a charming riverside patio ‚Äî great paninis and moonshine pork BBQ plate. Peaceful Side Social is newer with good craft beer. For a special lunch, drive 15 minutes to Amico Delicatessen in Maryville for authentic Italian sandwiches.
Farewell dinner
Last night of the trip! If you didn't get to Dancing Bear Bistro yesterday, tonight's the night (reservations required). Otherwise, Riverstone or Little River Pub for a casual send-off.
8

Return to South Mississippi
Drive home to South Mississippi. Approximately 400-450 miles, 6.5-7.5 hours driving time (plan 8-9 hours with breaks).
Breakfast at Highland Manor
Enjoy the free continental breakfast with homemade muffins before hitting the road.
Route home
US-321 W to Maryville ‚Üí I-140 W to I-40/75 W ‚Üí I-40 W to Nashville ‚Üí I-65 S to Birmingham ‚Üí I-59 S to South Mississippi. Can bypass downtown Nashville via I-440 loop.
Break Stop: Knoxville area
About 45 minutes from Townsend. I-40 Exit 373-380. Good breakfast stop if you skipped the hotel breakfast. Cracker Barrel, Waffle House available. Litton's Market on Kingston Pike has excellent burgers if you want a short detour.
Break Stop: Cookeville, TN
About 2.5 hours from Townsend. I-40 Exit 286-290. Good lunch stop with multiple options.
Break Stop: Nashville area / Columbia
About 4 hours from Townsend. Puckett's Grocery in Columbia (I-65 Exit 46) is a great Southern lunch if you're hungry. Good biscuits and meat-and-three plates.
Break Stop: Birmingham area
About 2.5 hours from Nashville. I-65 Exit 260-262. Final stretch stop for gas and restroom before the home stretch.
Arrive home
Welcome back to South Mississippi! Rest up and enjoy the memories.
