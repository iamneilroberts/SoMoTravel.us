Glacier Bay National Park - Scenic Cruising

July 11, 2026 (Saturday)
Arrival â–¼
The crown jewel of your Alaska cruise! The Eurodam spends the entire day cruising through Glacier Bay National Park, one of the most spectacular wilderness areas on Earth. Watch massive tidewater glaciers calve into the sea, spot humpback whales and sea otters, and listen to National Park Rangers narrate the experience. This is the day everyone remembers.
Arrival
The Edgewater Hotel
2411 Alaskan Way, Pier 67, Seattle, WA 98121
Check-in: 3:00 PM
Check-out: 11:00 AM
6:00 AM
National Park Rangers Board the Ship Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
Glacier Bay National Park rangers board the ship at the entrance to the bay. They provide commentary throughout the day from the bow, observation deck, and via PA system. Only ships with special permits can enter Glacier Bay.
8:00 AM
South Marble Islands - Sea Lions & Puffins Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
As the ship enters the bay, pass the Marble Islands where hundreds of Steller sea lions haul out on the rocks. Watch for tufted puffins, bald eagles, and other seabirds. Have binoculars ready!
10:00 AM
Johns Hopkins Glacier Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
The ship approaches Johns Hopkins Glacier, one of the most active tidewater glaciers in the park. It's 12 miles long and 250 feet tall at its face. The deep blue color and thunderous calving are unforgettable.
11:30 AM
Margerie Glacier (Main Event) Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
The ship slowly turns in front of Margerie Glacier, spending approximately 1 hour here. This glacier is 21 miles long and its face towers 250 feet above the water and extends 100 feet below. Watch and listen for calving - massive chunks of ice crashing into the sea with thunderous booms. The captain rotates the ship so all sides get a front-row view.
12:30 PM
Grand Pacific Glacier Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
Adjacent to Margerie, the Grand Pacific Glacier marks the US-Canada border. It's a rock-covered glacier that looks very different from the bright blue Margerie. Rangers explain how glaciers advance and retreat.
2:00 PM
Junior Ranger Program Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
Diego and Sofia can participate in the Glacier Bay Junior Ranger program run by the National Park rangers on board. Complete the activity booklet and earn a Glacier Bay Junior Ranger badge - a wonderful keepsake! Available at the ranger station set up in the ship's lounge.
3:00 PM
Whale Watching from Deck Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
The exit route through the lower bay is prime whale watching territory. Humpback whales feed here in summer. The bridge crew and rangers announce sightings. Sea otters, harbor seals on ice floes, and mountain goats on the cliffs are also commonly spotted.
7:00 PM
Ranger Evening Presentation Included
Part of your cruise package - no extra cost
The park rangers give an evening lecture about Glacier Bay's ecosystem, climate change impacts, and conservation efforts before disembarking the ship. A thought-provoking and educational end to an incredible day.
Dining
Highlights
  • Margerie Glacier calving - massive ice chunks falling into the sea
  • National Park Rangers providing expert narration all day
  • Wildlife: humpback whales, sea lions, puffins, sea otters, bald eagles
  • Junior Ranger badge opportunity for the kids
  • 360-degree pristine mountain and glacier wilderness
Tips
  • This is the ONE day to wake up early - be on deck by 7:00 AM for the full experience
  • Dress in warm layers - it's noticeably colder near the glaciers (40-50Â°F at the glacier face)
  • The forward observation areas on Decks 6 and 7 have the best unobstructed views
  • Bring your best camera - this is the most photographed day of the cruise
  • Glacier Bay Gala Night is one of two formal nights - pack accordingly
  • Listen for the 'crack and rumble' of calving glaciers - the sound carries for miles

Dining

Hidden Gems

Steep Creek Bear & Salmon Viewing

Near Mendenhall Glacier, Juneau

A free viewing platform where you can watch black bears catching spawning salmon just feet away. July is peak salmon season, and bears are frequently spotted from the boardwalk. Much closer wildlife viewing than most paid excursions offer.

Pro Tip: Visit between 9 AM and 2 PM for the best bear activity. Stay quiet and keep distance - bring a zoom lens for photos.

Smuggler's Cove Rainforest Trail

Ketchikan, AK

A short, easy boardwalk trail through stunning temperate rainforest just south of town. Giant Sitka spruce trees, lush ferns, and sometimes deer. Almost no tourists compared to Creek Street, and completely free.

Pro Tip: Take the local bus ($2) south to Rotary Beach and walk the trail from there. About 30 minutes round trip.

Gold Rush Cemetery & Reid Falls

Skagway, AK

A 15-minute walk north of Broadway Street leads to the historic Gold Rush Cemetery where Soapy Smith and Frank Reid are buried. Behind the cemetery, a short trail leads to the beautiful 300-foot Reid Falls waterfall. Free and rarely crowded.

Pro Tip: Read about the Soapy Smith vs. Frank Reid gunfight story before visiting - it makes the cemetery much more interesting for kids!

Bartlett Cove Beach Walk at Glacier Bay

Glacier Bay (from the ship)

While everyone crowds the bow for glacier views, head to the aft (back) deck during scenic cruising for a completely different perspective. The aft wake view with mountains reflected in the calm fjord water is magical and crowd-free.

Pro Tip: Best photos come from the aft pool deck (Deck 9) where you get the ship's wake trail leading toward the mountains.

Alaska's northern latitude means extended twilight in July, but on clear nights the star viewing from the upper decks is spectacular with zero light pollution. The ship's naturalist sometimes hosts informal stargazing sessions.

Best time: After 11 PM on sea days. Best viewing from the Sports Deck (Deck 11) away from pool lights.

A free 9-acre outdoor sculpture park along Seattle's waterfront, just north of Pier 67 near The Edgewater Hotel. Walk among large-scale art installations with stunning views of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.

Best time: Early morning or sunset for the best light and fewer crowds.

Best time: Early morning for a peaceful soak before breakfast, or during scenic cruising for jaw-dropping views.

Cruise Packing Tips

Must-Have Essentials

  • Passports or REAL ID for all 4 travelers
  • Waterproof rain jacket (lightweight, packable)
  • Warm fleece or layering mid-layer
  • Binoculars for wildlife spotting
  • Comfortable walking shoes (waterproof preferred)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ (July sun reflects off glaciers)
  • Reusable water bottles
  • Motion sickness remedies (Dramamine or Sea-Bands)
  • Power strip for cabin charging (limited outlets)
  • Formal night attire (1-2 gala evenings)

Recommended

  • Camera with zoom lens for wildlife (or phone with zoom)
  • Insulated gloves for Glacier Bay day
  • Warm hat and scarf for glacier viewing
  • Backpack daypack for excursions
  • Zip-lock bags for phone/electronics in rain
  • Kids' activity books and cards for sea days
  • Magnetic hooks for stateroom organization (cabin walls are metal!)
  • Highlighter for daily ship newsletter
  • Small bills for tipping porters and tour guides
  • Wrinkle-release spray for formal night clothes

Leave at Home

The ship provides these, or they take up unnecessary space:

  • Beach gear and flip-flops (not a tropical cruise)
  • Hairdryers (provided in stateroom)
  • Towels (ship and excursion providers supply them)
  • Heavy winter coats (layers work better than bulk)
  • Too many formal outfits (only 1-2 formal nights)
  • Expensive jewelry (casual cruise atmosphere)
